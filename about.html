Camera-V is a media notary service that attempts to verify and notarize images and video that have been created with the Camera-V app or any app that supports the J3M standard.

How is this a Notary?

When someone in our Camera-V club takes a photo or video with the app, they submit it here. Our verification server reads the J3M metadata in order to deduce the provenance of the media. In our system, provenance is granted if:

  1. the hash of pixels in the image or video matches the hash declared in the J3M metadata bundle
  2. the J3M metadata bundle in the media is properly signed by the photographer's PGP key

Once provenance is established, someone at Camera-V notarizes the document by sending the SHA-256 hash representation of the J3M metadata to the Bitcoin blockchain using our corresponding Proof of Existence server. Once actual Bitcoin is paid to the resulting address, the media can be considered notarized, and we automatically generate a notarization announcement.

You can verify the PGP message you see at the bottom of any notary announcement by downloading the PGP message, and decrypting it using your GPG tool of choice using our GPG key.

We use version control to keep permanent record of these notary announcements. In addition to finding these annoucements here, you can find them on our github page. Every once in awhile, we will commit all new annoucements to a repository where they can be audited publicly.

What is proof.camera-v.org?

That's where we host our Proof of Existence server.

What is j3m.camera-v.org?

This is where we index, analyze, and perform queries on the metadata attached to submissions.
